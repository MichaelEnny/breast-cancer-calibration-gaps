My research focuses on designing and evaluating trustworthy AI for clinical decision support — covering disease prediction, emergency triage, and ICU monitoring. The key question I'm answering is: how do we know a healthcare AI system is actually safe and reliable in the real world, not just on a test set?

---

## Full Research Area Statement (Formal Introduction or Bio)

My research develops evaluation frameworks, validated metrics, and open-source tools for assessing whether clinical AI systems are trustworthy enough for real-world deployment. The core question I answer is: how do we know a healthcare AI system is actually safe, calibrated, fair, and reliable in production — not just on a test set?

I address this question by introducing new measurement tools (including metrics for probability calibration equity, cross-site subgroup vulnerability, and composite deployment readiness) and by building validated infrastructure that other researchers and clinical teams can apply directly. Security and governance are treated as integral components of deployment trustworthiness, not as a separate concern bolted on after model development.

I apply and validate this work across three clinical domains: disease prediction (chronic kidney disease, breast cancer), emergency department triage, and ICU risk monitoring — using large credentialed datasets including MIMIC-IV-ED and the eICU Collaborative Research Database. The clinical domains are the validation context; the evaluation frameworks and tools are the contribution.

---

## Key Phrase

**Trustworthy healthcare AI evaluation** — specifically probability calibration, demographic fairness, failure detection, and deployment readiness, which distinguishes this work from research that builds prediction models without assessing whether those models are safe to deploy.

---

## Dissertation Thesis (One Sentence)

A trustworthy clinical AI system requires not just strong predictive performance, but validated reliability, calibration equity, adversarial robustness, and governance readiness — and this dissertation delivers the metrics, tools, and framework to measure and achieve all four.
