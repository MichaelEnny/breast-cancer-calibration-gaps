Please find enclosed our manuscript, "Governing Trustworthy Agentic Healthcare AI: A Calibration-First Framework with Distributed Ledger Accountability," submitted for consideration in the special issue on **Ethics of AI Agents and Agentic AI in Healthcare**.

This perspective paper addresses a structural governance gap at the intersection of clinical AI deployment and distributed ledger accountability. As healthcare AI systems transition from single-inference prediction tools to autonomous, multi-step agentic pipelines, existing governance frameworks — including TRIPOD+AI, DECIDE-AI, and the FDA AI/ML SaMD guidance — leave critical accountability requirements unaddressed. Specifically, these frameworks do not specify how to measure reliability at the level of individual agent decision steps, how to verify that probabilistic outputs are well-calibrated throughout an agentic pipeline, how to maintain patient consent for autonomous AI actions across care episodes, or how to produce audit records that are tamper-resistant and independently verifiable.

This paper proposes a five-layer trustworthiness governance framework that directly addresses these gaps: a reliability evaluation layer using calibration metrics and conformal prediction uncertainty quantification; a fairness auditing layer for subgroup calibration equity; a provenance verification layer for model lineage and training data documentation; a patient consent management layer for autonomous AI action; and a deployment readiness scoring layer with ongoing monitoring criteria. Crucially, we argue that distributed ledger technologies provide the accountability infrastructure — immutable audit trails, smart contract-encoded ethical constraints, decentralized consent registries, and on-chain model registries — that makes each layer operationally enforceable and independently verifiable rather than merely aspirational.
